RKB: Rigid bodies

A body is rigid if all lengths and angles formed from points on the body remain constant over time. That is, if we choose three points \(A, B, C\) on the body, then the lengths \(AB\), \(BC\), and \(AC\) must remain fixed, and the angles \(\angle ABC\), \(\angle BCA\), and \(\angle CAB\) remain fixed.
